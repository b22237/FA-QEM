Abstract

The rapid growth of 3D content from modern reconstruction and generative pipelines — neural rendering, large-scale asset generation — has produced an abundance of dense, noisy, and often non-manifold meshes. While these representations achieve high visual fidelity, their complexity poses significant challenges for simulation, AR/VR, and scientific computing, where efficient and reliable geometry is essential.

We propose Feature-Aware Quadric Error Metric (FA-QEM), a comprehensive mesh simplification pipeline for modern 3D assets. Our approach introduces a novel multi-term quadric error formulation that jointly encodes geometric deviation, boundary curvature, and surface normal consistency — enabling optimal vertex placement that preserves sharp features even under aggressive simplification.

We further show that high-fidelity geometric simplification significantly improves downstream appearance transfer, serving as a superior front-end for texture mapping via successive mapping. Extensive evaluations on AI-generated meshes and large-scale real-world datasets (Thingi10K, Real-World Textured Things) demonstrate consistently lower geometric error, better visual fidelity, and substantially faster runtimes — while maintaining robustness across diverse, challenging inputs.
